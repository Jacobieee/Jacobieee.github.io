Multi-future Trajectory Prediction with Deep Learning Techniques (Honours Thesis)
University of New South Wales Feb. 2021 - Dec. 2021
• Predict multiple plausible future pedestrian trajectories in deep learning models.
• Developed a multi-scale graph-based spatial transformer with attention mechanism and Graph Attention Network.
• Designed a trajectory smoothing algorithm “Memory Replay” to predict temporally consistent trajectories.
• Proposed a new metric to evaluate the comprehensiveness of predicted multiple trajectories.
• The result of the model achieved the state-of-the-art performance on the datasets used. This work attended the CSE Thesis
Showcase 2022 and has been accepted by CVPR 2022. Github

Identification and Localization of COVID-19 Abnormalities on Chest Radiography
University of New South Wales Jun. 2021 - Aug. 2021
• Diagnosis of human chest X-ray images by deep learning and machine learning techniques. This project was conducted in
COMP9491, Applied AI, UNSW.
• Reproduced the paper “Deep Disentangled Generative Model (DGM)” with another teammate for data enhancement (to
augment lung opacity by decomposing images into synthetic normal images and disentangled residue map).
• Used the Siamese Networks (few-shot learning) with ML based classifiers and clusterers to classify the images into diagnoses.
• Learned a variety of Generative Adversarial Networks as well as improved the implementation skills of TensorFlow. Github
